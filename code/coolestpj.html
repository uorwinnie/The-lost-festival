Thingyan Festival,
Water Festival

Thingyan is a water festival that is held annually, typically during the second or third week of April, to mark the Myanmar New Year. Thingyan is a word derived from Sanskrit, that means change and accordingly, the Thingyan Festival marks the changing of the old year to the new.

In the Burmese calendar, which is a lunar calendar, it is a movable feast,ut it is flexible in the solar year,which explains the fact that wheras it falls on different Burmese dates in different years, it falls usually during the period 13 th to 16 th April and very occasionally during the period 13 th to 17 th April or 14th to 16 th April in the western solar calendar.

First day of Thingyan days is called “A Kyo”, the day that starts the various kinds of religious activities and which follows by the second day “A Kya”, third day “A Kyat” and the final day as “A Tat”. In the leap year, there is one extra day of “A Kyat” so there will be two “A Kyat” days. After these four or five days, the Myanmar lunar calendar changes to very first day of New Year. Thingyan holidays are the most important public holidays and the school year ends for all students & the summer holidays begin.

The festival features many traditional rituals and customs, including the splashing of water and dousing each other in water as a way of cleansing the old year's sins and welcoming the new year with joy and happiness. Although Thingyan is a religious festival basically, its main feature is water-throwing.
